JEJU

Overview

Jeju City is the largest city on Jeju Island, South Korea’s most popular vacation destination.
Known for its volcanic landscapes, picturesque beaches, and unique culture, Jeju is often called the “Hawaii of Korea.

Attractions

  • Hallasan National Park

    • Home to Hallasan Mountain, South Korea’s highest peak. A great place for hiking with stunning views.

  • Jeju Loveland

    • An outdoor sculpture park featuring erotic works of art, making it a unique and quirky destination.

  • Seongsan Ilchulbong

    • A volcanic crater offering a scenic sunrise view over the ocean. It’s a UNESCO World Heritage site.

  • Jeju Folk Village

    • A traditional village that recreates life on Jeju Island in the past, with cultural exhibits and events.

  • Manjanggul Cave

    • One of the longest lava tubes in the world, known for its impressive rock formations.

Accommodations

  • Shilla Stay Jeju

    • A chic and modern hotel located in the heart of Jeju City.

  • Jeju Bom Stay

    • A boutique hotel offering great views and comfortable accommodations.

  • Lotte Hotel Jeju

    • A luxury resort with extensive facilities including an outdoor pool, spa, and casino.
